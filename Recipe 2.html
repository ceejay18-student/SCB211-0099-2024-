Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 diced tomatoes
  • Cream cheese cube
  • Mayonnaise
  • Minced meat
  • Chilli(optional but advised)

    For the Dough:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Approximately ½ cup water (adjust as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 500g minced beef or chicken
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced and steamed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 2 handfuls of broccoli, chopped and steamed
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (dhania), chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Cream cheese (optional)

Method of Preparation:

Preparing the Dough:

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Add the vegetable oil and mix well.
  3. Gradually add water while mixing until a smooth dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it becomes elastic.
  5. Divide the dough into 6 equal balls.
  6. Coat each ball with vegetable oil, place them in a bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let them rest for at least 2 hours.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, add a tablespoon of vegetable oil.
  2. Add the minced meat and cook until browned.
  3. Add the garlic paste, chopped onion, and diced green bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Add the diced carrot, peas, corn, and broccoli to the mixture.
  5. Season with black pepper, crumbled chicken bouillon cube (if using), and salt to taste.
  6. Cook the mixture until all ingredients are well combined and cooked through.
  7. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Assembling the Swahili Pizza:

  1. On a lightly oiled surface, take one dough ball and roll it out into a very thin circle, approximately 10 inches in diameter.
  2. Place a generous spoonful of the filling mixture in the center of the dough.
  3. Crack an egg over the filling.
  4. Add a dollop of mayonnaise and a small amount of cream cheese if desired.
  5. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to create a square packet, ensuring all sides are sealed.

Cooking the Swahili Pizza:

  1. Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place the prepared dough packet seam-side down into the skillet.
  3. Cook for 5-8 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Once cooked, remove from the skillet and let it rest on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  5. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls and filling.
  6. Serving Suggestion: Serve the Swahili pizzas hot, optionally drizzled with additional mayonnaise or ketchup. Enjoy your meal!
